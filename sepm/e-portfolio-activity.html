As a Project Manager, managing the emotional reactions of a customer is crucial in ensuring a positive user experience. Emotional reactions, described by Van der Linden et al. (2019), refer to the feelings, moods, and attitudes a user experiences while interacting with a product or service. It is crucial to comprehend the variables that affect emotional responses and create solutions for them.

One key factor is the design of the product or service. Hassenzahl et al. (2003) found that the design can evoke emotions through sensory and aesthetic experiences. For instance, using bright colours, bold fonts, and clean lines can evoke positive emotions, while using dark colours, small fonts, and cluttered layouts can evoke negative emotions. It is vital to consider the design and ensure it evokes positive emotions.

Another factor is the user's perception of control. Hassenzahl et al. (2010) found that users are more likely to have positive emotional reactions when they feel they have control over the product or service. On the other hand, users are more likely to have adverse emotional reactions when they feel they do not have control. As a Project Manager, it is crucial to provide users with the necessary level of control.

The context in which a product or a service is used can also influence emotional reactions, as found by Kort et al. (2001). For example, a product used in a stressful or anxious situation may evoke negative emotions, while a product used in a relaxed or positive situation may evoke positive emotions; hence considering the context and design of a product or a service accordingly should be vital to a project manager.

Finally, the level of personalization of the product or service can also influence emotional reactions. Hassenzahl et al. (2010) found that users are more likely to have positive emotional reactions when they feel the product or service is personalized to their needs and preferences and negative emotional reactions when they feel it is not personalized.

In conclusion, the design of a product or a service, a user's perception of control, experience, context, and personalization are key factors that influence emotional reactions.
