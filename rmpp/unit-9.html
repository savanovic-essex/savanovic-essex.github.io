Research Methods and Professional Practice

Unit 9

Collaborative Discussion 2: Case Study: Accuracy of information

Summary Post

In the case study of Abi, a researcher and statistical programmer, ethical concerns arise regarding the presentation and potential misuse of the data collected on Whizzz, a new cereal. The American Statistical Association (ASA) Ethical Guidelines for Statistical Practice emphasise unbiased and transparent work (American Statistical Association, 2018). Thus, Abi must present both positive and negative analyses of Whizzz, ensuring the manufacturer has access to complete and accurate information (Bailer & Kimber, 2016).

Abi is responsible for the use of his program results to some extent. If he knows that his work may be misused, he has an ethical obligation to address these concerns with the manufacturer and encourage responsible use of the information (Resnik & Shamoo, 2011). However, if Abi anticipates that the manufacturer will only publicise the positive results, he could discuss his concerns with them and emphasise the importance of transparent reporting (Chartres et al., 2019; Cullerton et al., 2019). He could also explore other channels for sharing the complete results, such as submitting the findings to a peer-reviewed journal or presenting at a conference (Mann, 2002).

Additional strategies for maintaining research data integrity include data management plans (Tenopir et al., 2011), blinding and randomisation (Pannucci & Wilkins, 2010), reproducibility and replication (Herndon et al., 2014), preregistration (Nosek et al., 2018), and open access and peer review (McKiernan et al., 2016).

In conclusion, Abi should adhere to ethical guidelines and present positive and negative analyses, ensuring that the manufacturer understands the data comprehensively. By adhering to ethical guidelines and presenting both positive and negative analyses, Abi can ensure that the manufacturer understands the data comprehensively. Additionally, addressing concerns about the potential misuse of his findings and exploring alternative avenues for disseminating accurate information will uphold research integrity and foster transparency.
