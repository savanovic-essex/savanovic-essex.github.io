Unit 7

Collaborative Discussion 2: Case Study: Accuracy of information

Initial Post

According to the American Statistical Association (ASA) Ethical Guidelines for Statistical Practice (2018), statisticians must act with integrity and ensure their work is unbiased and transparent. Abi must consider these guidelines as he decides how to present his findings.

It would be unethical for Abi to only present analyses that cast Whizzz in a favourable light, as this would be a biased representation of the data (Bailer & Kimber, 2016). Instead, Abi should present both the positive and negative analyses, ensuring that the manufacturer has access to complete and accurate information. This approach aligns with the ASA guidelines, emphasising the importance of communicating any analyses' limitations and assumptions (American Statistical Association, 2018).

Abi is responsible for the use of his program results to some extent. If he knows that his work may be misused, he has an ethical obligation to address these concerns with the manufacturer and encourage responsible use of the information (Resnik & Shamoo, 2011).

If Abi anticipates that the manufacturer will only publicise the positive results, he could consider discussing his concerns with them and emphasising the importance of transparent reporting. Additionally, he could explore other channels for sharing the complete results, such as submitting the findings to a peer-reviewed journal or presenting at a conference. These actions could mitigate the potential for biased reporting and ensure the public can access accurate information about Whizzz.

In conclusion, Abi should adhere to ethical guidelines and present positive and negative analyses, ensuring that the manufacturer understands the data comprehensively. He should also address any concerns about the potential misuse of his findings and explore alternative avenues for disseminating accurate information.
